Chapter 3. Pocket it and save for later

[I grimaced at the soiled bread before stashing it in my pocket. Fox ignored me before turning away.]

‘Bread mould is the best mould,’ he laughed, biting into his own portion.
‘Happy tallyday!’
Smiling through his mouthful, Fox tapped the small pouch he wore strapped to his leg, hidden beneath loose-fitting shorts. Instead of the all too familiar silence or occasional clatter of wooden coins there was a dull metallic jingle.
Bread and bruises forgotten. I stared wide-eyed. ‘Actual metal coins? Irons? Coppers?’
‘Both!’ Fox replied, his smile widening further. ‘I honestly don’t know what those idiots were thinking, they couldn’t even think of a better place to hide it than the bedroll.
Aviary looked up from the careful portioning of her chunk of bread.
‘Only you two could smile in a place like this,’ she chided, ignoring her own smile at the news. ‘Do you want to lose it all?’
‘Actually, I want to try again,’ Fox said with a knowing smile. ‘Tallyday’ beatings are the best distractions we get and nobody has died from one in ages,’
‘But we’ve already had our beatings?’ I replied, returning my attention to the bread and trying to ignore the pain that streaked through me with each movement.
‘Now that I’ve been booted from the safe-house I get no more protection or handouts. Nobody will try anything with me looking like this.’
‘Well, yeah,’ my friend droned. ‘I’m not that thick. That’s why I’m stealing that rag round your arm. A blood stain like that only comes from a tally cut; nobody will hesitate to beat me senseless if I wear it. One of you just has to rob ‘em blind while they’re at it.’
There was little protest, we all figured that tally beatings were mostly for the show and the reward was worth the risk.
