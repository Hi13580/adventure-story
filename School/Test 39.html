Chapter 8. A

Filth engulfed me, as I fought to resist the vile stench that threatened to force its way into my lungs. The thick layer of waste and grime reformed quickly and to those on the bank I became part of it, fading into the muck.

Finally given the chance my body screamed, reminding me with agonising detail of every bruise and ache. My shoulders stung, jaw throbbed, legs burned and the tally on my arm had re-opened; I prayed the rag would be enough to save it from rot. I cursed my own stupidity as I lay in the dying river, feeling the filth seep into my skin. The man was right, forgetting to listen was almost unforgivable. I cursed myself again.
