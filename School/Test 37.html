Chapter 8. B

Only now had I noticed the crimson blood trailing down from my thigh to my leg. I ripped my trouser pants apart to create an even bigger gap, taking out the bloodied and shredded chunk of bread that took the dagger’s damage. I tied the bloody rag over the open wound across my thigh before untying the pouch from the hidden pocket in my trouser pants.

‘Some iron, seven coppers and two silvers.’ I looked at the now even bloodier rag at my thigh. ‘Hardly worth it for what I went through. Do you think it’s enough for a piece of our own land?’

‘Hardly,’ Fox spoke up.

‘Well? What now?’ I was full of questions.

We sat in silence before Fox once again spoke up. ‘We need more than what we got if we wanna survive. I think we should separate for now and scout for more plots, maybe even one that we could call our own.

There was a comfortable hum between the three of us. We lapsed into a silent agreement as we enjoyed the last night of the friendship we would sorely miss in the days to come.]
